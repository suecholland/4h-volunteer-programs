4‑H Volunteer Programs

Building skills, confidence, and community through hands‑on learning

About These Programs

I volunteer with 4‑H to help youth develop practical life skills, civic awareness, and confidence. This page highlights current programs I lead and serves as a home base for future 4‑H projects.

Civic Engagement Program

This program introduces youth to civic responsibility, leadership, and community involvement in age-appropriate and engaging ways.

Sessions are discussion-based and may be held at the Farm Bureau or another approved meeting space.

  • Understanding community roles and responsibilities
  • Basic civics and how local government works
  • Service projects and respectful dialogue
  • Leadership and teamwork skills

Young Cooking Class

A hands-on cooking program designed to teach youth safe, healthy, and practical cooking skills they can use at home.

Cooking sessions may take place in a kitchen-equipped setting, which may include the Farm Bureau or a home kitchen, depending on the lesson.

  • Kitchen safety and basic food handling
  • Simple, nutritious recipes
  • Reading recipes and measuring ingredients
  • Building confidence and independence

Program Locations

Programs are typically held at the local Farm Bureau facility. Some sessions may also take place at my home, depending on the activity and availability.

All locations are selected with youth safety, supervision, and appropriate facilities in mind. Specific location details will always be shared with parents or guardians in advance.

Future Programs

This page is designed to grow. Additional 4‑H programs, workshops, or seasonal activities can easily be added here in the future.

Examples may include:

  • Financial literacy
  • STEM or project‑based learning
  • Public speaking or leadership workshops

Safety & Supervision

All activities are age-appropriate and supervised in accordance with 4-H volunteer guidelines. Parents and guardians will receive advance information about activities, locations, and any materials needed.
